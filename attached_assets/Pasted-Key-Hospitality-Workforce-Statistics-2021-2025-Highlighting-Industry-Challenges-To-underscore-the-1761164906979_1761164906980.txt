Key Hospitality Workforce Statistics (2021–2025) Highlighting Industry Challenges

To underscore the need for innovative workforce and career platforms in hospitality, here are three of the most compelling recent statistics. Each reveals a critical pain point – from rampant turnover to stagnant career growth and low engagement – that such solutions can help address:

Sky-High Turnover – ~74% vs. 13%: The hospitality sector experiences an annual employee turnover around 74%, far above the ~13% average across U.S. industries
hitec.org
. In other words, hotels and restaurants must replace nearly three-quarters of their staff each year, a churn rate that dramatically drives up hiring and training costs and undermines service quality (Source: Deloitte “Future of Frontline Workforce” report, 2024).

Lack of Career Growth – 42% Plan to Leave: In a 2023 industry survey, over two in five hospitality employees (42%) said they intend to leave the hospitality field for a different career path
go.harri.com
. This high exit intent signals that many workers see limited career progression or development opportunities in hospitality and feel undervalued. Without clearer growth pathways and professional development, a huge portion of talent is at risk of attrition (Source: CGA by NielsenIQ & Harri “Hospitality Talent Challenges” survey, 2023).

Low Engagement & Connectivity – Only 31% Engaged: Only 31% of hospitality workers report being fully engaged in their jobs, while the majority are “quiet quitting” or actively disengaged
global.hsmai.org
. Such widespread disengagement – driven by employees feeling underappreciated and disconnected from leadership – points to poor morale and communication. This highlights a lack of connectivity on the frontlines and the need for better engagement and communication tools to value staff (Source: Gallup North America Workforce data, 2023).

Each of these statistics underscores a facet of the hospitality workforce crisis: unprecedented turnover, stunted career development, and disengaged, disconnected employees. Together, they make a compelling case for new workforce and career platforms that can improve retention by creating clear career pathways, boost engagement by recognizing and connecting staff, and leverage technology to better support and empower hospitality employees.
